Material cost fundamentals

Understanding filament cost per gram is the foundation of accurate 3D printing pricing. Whether you're a hobbyist or running a small business, knowing your material cost helps you avoid losses and set profitable prices.

How to Calculate

Cost per gram = Filament price per kg ÷ 1000

Example: $22/kg ÷ 1000 = $0.022/g

If your print uses 150 grams: 150 × $0.022 = $3.30

Why It Matters

Even a few cents difference per gram can significantly impact profitability at scale. A print using 200g of Nylon vs PLA can differ by $7 in material cost alone — that's before electricity, labor, and waste.

Tips to Reduce Material Cost

Optimize infill percentage (15–20% is often enough for non-structural parts), enable adaptive layers, and use gyroid infill which provides great strength-to-weight ratio. Track spool waste from failed prints in your pricing.
