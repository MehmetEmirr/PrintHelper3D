Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate 3D printing cost?

Add up: (filament grams × price/gram) + electricity (wattage × hours × kWh rate) + machine wear + labor. Apply your profit margin % on top. PrintCalc handles all of this automatically.

How much should I charge for a 3D print?

Total cost × (1 + profit margin). For a $5 cost print with 50% margin, charge $7.50. Use Advanced mode to include platform fees, packaging, and waste.

What is PLA filament cost per gram?

PLA typically costs $15–$25 per kg — that is $0.015–$0.025 per gram. A 150g print at $22/kg costs about $3.30 in filament alone.

How much electricity does a 3D printer use?

Most FDM printers use 100–300W. A 150W printer running 5 hours = 0.75 kWh. At $0.15/kWh, that is about $0.11. Enter your actual wattage in Standard mode.

What is a good profit margin for 3D printing?

Most sellers aim for 40–100% profit margin. 50% is a solid starting point. Custom or engineering parts can command 100–200%. Always include all costs before calculating margin.
