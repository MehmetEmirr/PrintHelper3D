The Formula

Electricity cost = (Watts ÷ 1000) × hours × kWh rate

Example: A 150W printer running for 5 hours at $0.15/kWh: (150 ÷ 1000) × 5 × $0.15 = $0.11

Tips to Reduce Electricity Costs

Print at night if your utility offers time-of-use pricing — off-peak rates can be 30–50% lower.

Combine small prints into one plate. Each separate print has startup energy. Batching 4 small parts into one print cuts idle overhead by 75%.

Enclosures help maintain temperature, reducing how hard your heated bed has to work — especially for ABS and ASA.

Enable "sleep mode" or "eco mode" on printers that support it. Some printers idle at full power.
